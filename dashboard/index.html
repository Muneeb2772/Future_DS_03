§ 01

The five stages of a phone campaign

From initial contact list to signed subscription. Drop-off at every stage tells us where effort is wasted.

The headline number — 11.7% conversion on term-deposit subscriptions — hides a far more interesting story once the journey is broken into stages.

The biggest leak isn't at the end. It's Stage 1 → Stage 2, where 13,020 clients (28.8%) are marked with an unknown contact channel. These are people the campaign technically "targeted" but never actually reached through a tracked channel.

After that, engagement holds well (90%+), but the true filter is call quality. Once a real conversation happens, conversion probability is driven almost entirely by duration and prior-campaign history — not demographics.

KEY OBSERVATION
"The funnel loses more clients to channel failure than to rejection. Fix the pipe before worrying about the pitch."

§ 05

Five drop-off insights that matter

Each finding is defensible from the data alone and converts directly to an intervention.
INSIGHT 01

Channel attribution is broken for 1 in 3 contacts

13,020 clients (28.8%) have contact type "unknown." The conversion rate for this cohort is drastically lower than cellular/telephone. This is a data-capture failure, not necessarily a rejection — but it behaves like one at the analysis layer.
→ 28.8% of top-of-funnel wasted
INSIGHT 02

Call duration is the single strongest predictor

Calls under 60 seconds convert at 0.19%. Calls over 10 minutes convert at 46%. Calls over 20 minutes convert at 61.6%. This isn't causation (long calls happen because the client is interested), but it is a brutal diagnostic signal for lead quality.
→ 324× conversion gap across duration buckets
INSIGHT 03

Campaign fatigue starts at call #2

First-contact conversion is 14.6%. Second call drops to 11.2%. By the 5th call it's 7.9%, and by the 10th it's 4.2%. Calling the same client more than 3 times in one campaign is statistically counterproductive.
→ 71% relative decline from call 1 to call 10
INSIGHT 04

Seasonality multiplies conversion 5×

March (52.0%), December (46.7%), September (46.5%), October (43.8%) convert at 4–5× the May baseline. May alone accounts for 30% of all contacts but has the worst conversion. The campaign is running on the wrong calendar.
→ Reallocating volume from May → Mar/Oct could 2–3× conversions
INSIGHT 05

Prior success is a flashing green light

Clients who subscribed in a previous campaign convert at 64.7% — a 5.5× lift over baseline. Yet they represent only 3.3% of the contact list. This is the lowest-CAC segment by a wide margin and is being massively under-prioritized.
→ 64.7% conversion vs 11.7% baseline
INSIGHT 06

Age creates a "bathtub" — the middle is dead

Under-25s convert at 25.6%, over-65s at 42.1%, but the 35-54 bracket converts at only ~9%. Working-age prime earners are the hardest to convert to term deposits — likely because competing investment options are more attractive.
→ Middle-age conversion is HALF the baseline
§ 06

Actionable recommendations

Six interventions ranked by estimated impact on overall conversion rate.
01

Re-target previous-success clients with a dedicated campaign

The 1,511 clients with poutcome = success convert at 64.7%. Build a tiered nurture program for them: priority dialing queue, senior agent assignment, premium product offers. This is the lowest hanging fruit in the entire dataset.

EST. IMPACT
+8–12%on segment
on a 1,500-client base
02

Shift call volume out of May into March, September, October

May receives the highest call volume but the lowest conversion rate (~6-7%). Mar/Sep/Oct convert at 44–52%. Reallocating even 20% of May's volume to these months — without adding budget — should multiply conversions materially.

EST. IMPACT
+30–40%total conversions
zero-cost reallocation
03

Cap campaign contacts at 3 per client

Calls 4+ have diminishing and eventually negative returns. After the 3rd attempt, route the client to a different channel (SMS, email, in-branch) rather than keep dialing. Expect a modest conversion lift and substantial agent-hour savings.

EST. IMPACT
-25%agent time wasted
on low-conversion calls
04

Fix contact-channel data capture (remove "unknown")

28.8% of records having "unknown" contact type is a data-pipeline issue as much as a marketing one. This cohort underperforms because we can't even confirm they were reached. Require channel logging at the CRM level — any unlogged dial should fail silently in analytics, not count as "contacted."

EST. IMPACT
+20%funnel-reach accuracy
better attribution overall
05

Prioritize students, retirees, and the over-65 segment

Students (28.7%), retirees (22.8%), and over-65s (42.1%) are high-converters with relatively little competition for their capital. Design tailored scripts and product bundles for each — CD-like products with no early-withdrawal penalties for retirees, starter-saver messaging for students.

EST. IMPACT
2–3×conversion
in targeted segments
06

Train agents to hold conversations past the 60-second mark

The cliff between <60s calls (0.19% conversion) and 60–180s calls (3.9%) is 20×. Script coaching focused on the first 60 seconds — opening line, qualifying question, permission to continue — is the highest-leverage agent-training investment available.

EST. IMPACT
+5–8%absolute conversion lift
across all segments
